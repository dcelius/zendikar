Welcome to Zendikar

Trap-riddled ruins can be found on every continent. Mysterious glyphs hint at truths long forgotten. Unspeakable monsters lurk in the quiet of these hidden monuments of a forgotten past. The ruins still emanate power, and both planeswalkers and local explorers alike will undergo great peril to reap their rewards. From the otherworldly hedrons that drift across the sky, to the wondrous power of Life Blooms, Zendikar has no shortage of adventure and mysteries. While the Roil prevents civilization from grabbing hold, the spirit of the people of Zendikar knows no bounds as they continue to dredge into the earth, searching for ancient secrets perhaps better left undiscovered.

A Forgotten History

Zendikar’s history is long, but mostly undocumented. Due to the extreme destructive powers of the Roil and numerous cataclysmic events in the past, very few civilizations survived long enough to leave a mark on history, with none of those civilizations still intact today. However, from the stories passed down by the people of Zendikar, history is broken up into roughly three ages. The first age had magnificent cities and technology, the ruins of which can still be found scattered across the plane. The second age saw the construction of the mysterious hedrons and Skyclaves, as well as the dominance of the Makindi Empire - from which the Kor are descended. And the third age is today - the time of the Roil. A time where any amount of civilization can be destroyed in an instant by natural phenomenon, where people explore ancient ruins in search of treasure and power, where the lands of Zendikar seem to be truly alive.

Hedrons

Large, floating polyhedron-shaped stones called hedrons litter the landscape and sky. No one knows for sure what they are, but their gravity-defying existence hints at the strange properties of all gravity and mana on the plane. These ancient, rune-carved monoliths are strewn across Zendikar. Up to ten miles long, some of these stones drift in the sky; others are buried in the ground, some whole, some broken.

The Roil

The Roil is the colloquial name for Zendikar’s periodic volatility of the earth and climate. This force changes the land and is the reason why the plane remains untamed and deadly. Because it is magical in nature, the Roil can affect biology as well as geology and meteorology. Some of the effects of the Roil are as follows:

Life Blooms

Sudden and meteoric eruptions of growth in both plant and animal life across a small region, as if Zendikar is trying to repair itself after the damage it caused.

Tornadoes and Dust Storms

Raging dust storms limiting all visibility and strong tornadoes tear across the earth, limiting the farming potential of the land.

Earthquakes and Tectonic Shifts

Massive earthquakes and dramatic tectonic shifts continuously destroy structures and reshape geological features.

Floods and Tidal Waves

Huge floods and tidal waves breach upon the coastlines, wiping away villages and refilling coastal basins.

Sinkholes

Random sinkholes can appear anywhere, swallowing up sections of the earth and anything on it.

Elementals

Perhaps the most dangerous aspect of the Roil, Zendikar itself can animate in the form of elementals.

Toxic Gas Geysers

Most common in Guul Draz, geysers of toxic gas can erupt spontaneously, severely damaging all nearby plant and animal life.

Fissures and Lava Flows

Sudden fissures can erupt across the land, cracking the earth asunder, and massive lava flows leave rivers of scorched earth in their path.

Rock Crags

Floating sections of rock and earth hang in the sky above Zendikar, returning back to earth in a spectacular fashion.

The Skyclaves

Most continents possess a Skyclave, a collapsed ruin of a once floating city, seemingly from the same age as the hedrons. Whatever magic once held them aloft is no longer - except for the Skyclave in Akoum which floats still. However, due to the floating rubble and hedrons surrounding it, the height at which it floats in the sky, and the nest of drakes and dragons which call it home, it remains mostly unexplored.

Societies Driven By Exploration

Thanks to the Roil and the cataclysms that brought all former civilizations on Zendikar to their abrupt end, Zendikar is a tapestry of ever-changing geography and hidden mysteries. Because of this, exploring unknown territory and searching for lost technologies are crucial to the Zendikari way of life. Most organized efforts of exploration on Zendikar originate from the few centers of civilization, particularly Sea Gate.

Academic institutions task explorers to draw accurate maps (which must be updated frequently), collect ancient artifacts for study, and obtain general knowledge of the world beyond the city walls. Wealthy merchants also invest in expeditions, hoping to multiply their wealth and power with the discovery of valuable natural resources, the estab- lishment of new trade routes, and the acquisition of items that can be sold to academic institutions. Successful explorers often use the proceeds of their first expeditions to fund later ones, cherishing the freedom that comes from serving no master but the call of discovery. Though humans often form the backbones of these expeditions, they typically incorporate adventurers of other races as well, drawing on the broad expertise of Zendikar's diverse peoples. Trail guides, porters, mountaineering experts, diviners, hunters, trappers, cartographers, mages, missionaries, scholars, mercenaries, smiths, healers, and beasts of burden can turn an expedition into a large caravan that's practically a self-contained community.

In Sea Gate, six so-called expeditionary houses launch regular excursions into the wilds for various purposes. Named for the destinations of their first expeditions, they are the Valakut House, the Pelakka Foundation, and the Akoum, Murasa, Tazeem, and Bala Ged Expeditionary Houses. The rivalries of the houses are legendary, and they sometimes sabotage each other's expeditions - but sometimes they will work together on truly unique contracts. Each maintains hostels in various settlements, which serve as resources for adventurers and explorers who need to hire guides or purchase supplies.

Races of Zendikar

A wide variety of peoples walk the plane of Zendikar, including Elves, Goblins, Humans, Kor, Merfolk, and Vampires - among others. Many of these races have custom stats for you to use, but if you prefer to just reflavor existing content, alternatives are provided.

Elves

Being very adaptable, it's no wonder the elves of Zendikar flourish in places where other races struggle. Most commonly found on Murasa and Bala Ged, elves are split into 3 distinct nations and many distant clans.

World of Zendikar

The expansive and wild plane of Zendikar contains 7 continents: Akoum, Bala Ged, Guul Draz, Murasa, Ondu, Sejiri, and Tazeem. With vastly different ecosystems, mysteries left unsolved, and ruins still undiscovered, each continent has its secrets. And they will not give up their secrets lightly.

Akoum

The largest and most geologically unstable continent. Basically a giant supervolcano, the terrain is covered in jagged rocks, boiling hot geysers, and hardened lava flows. Akoum is also home to the tallest and most expansive mountain range in Zendikar known as the Teeth.

  • Details

Bala Ged

Just south of Akoum and the western half of the greater Bala Draz continent, the terrain is a humid and thick rainforest, rife with deadly flora and fauna.

  • Details

Guul Draz

The eastern half of the greater Bala Draz continent, the terrain is a fetid swampland hostile to all life except the vampires who call this place home.

  • Details

Murasa

A small continent far to the east of Akoum, the terrain being an extremely lush jungle with untamed beasts and hidden treasures.

  • Details

Ondu

A large continent to the west of Akoum covered in dusty ravines and trenches, with the exception of the temperate Turntimber Forest. Ondu has 3 islands surrounding it, Agadeem, Beyeen, and Jawr.

  • Details

Sejiri

Located far to the north, the terrain of this polar continent is a frozen and desolate tundra where almost no life lives.

  • Details

Tazeem

A moderately sized continent to the southwest of Ondu, possessing both varying climates and varying peoples. Home to the Halimar and the famous Sea Gate, Tazeem is the center of trade in Zendikar.

Notable Settlements of Zendikar

The largest settlements on Zendikar would barely be considered notable on most other planes, but here, in this dangerous and tumultuous world, they are the pinnacle of civilization.

Malakir

Also known as the Dread City, Malakir is the pinnacle of vampiric culture. Split into 5 districts ruled by its namesake bloodchief, the Dread City is by far the largest settlement on Zendikar.

Sea Gate

The center of trade and learning on Zendikar, Sea Gate is a bastion of unity located on top of the ancient Halimar dam of Tazeem. Sea Gate also is home to the legendary Sea Gate Expeditionary Houses.

Goma Fada

Also known as the Walking City, Goma Fada is a massive caravan that follows a series of routes across Akoum every year

Coralhelm

Made of enormous conch shells suspended over the Umara river of Tazeem, Coralhelm the new capital of the merfolk and a haven of knowledge.

Zulaport

Located on Beyeen, off the coast of Ondu, Zulaport is a major trading hub constantly under threat from the volcanoes known as the Crown of Talib that make up Beyeen.

The Tumbled Palace

Located on Murasa, the Tumbled Palace is a massive tree that hangs over the ocean and is the ancestral home of the Tajuru elves.
